Practical Manual to Pranahuti Meditation

A simple and natural method leading the seeker towards the Ultimate Reality

This is a simple and natural method that helps the seeker in experiencing the Ultimate Reality. This experience brings down the divine-consciousness into the heart of the seeker. The experience of divine-consciousness gradually molds the behavioral personality due to divinization of the lower centers. He realizes that he is not just a physical, vital or mental being but a PURE CONSCIOUSNESS / AWARENESS itself. In the lap of this pure consciousness, he witnesses the creation, sustenance and dissolution of all phenomenal happenings. This comes micro-cosmically in the beginning, and later macro-cosmically. A subjective uncaused peace envelops the person which transcends all understandings. Our ancient scriptures called it "Immortality" ("Amrutatva"). The individual keeps oneself aside as a witness; performs all the works that come to his / her share with a high level of dexterity.

What is needed in this system of meditation is honesty to oneself and sincere desire to go beyond the material coverings of his personality.

Step 1

Choose a time in the morning convenient to you, say 6:00 am and 7:00 pm; keep to this time at all costs.

Step 2

Select a convenient place (6 sq ft where you are not disturbed. Do not change the place according to fancy; try to keep to the same place.

Step 3

Sit in a convenient posture—one that does not strain you physically; make the base of the posture broader than you feel; relaxes head, arms...

Step 4

Make a supplication that a divine light is glowing diffused in your heart. (A) not make mentally to say the left in the heart on we right.

Step 5

Slowly divert your inner towards a point in the heart—just before the finger widths of your left nipple—call it the point 'A'.

Step 6

Mentally repeat this prayer:

O! Master — Thou are the real Goal of Human life —
We are all yet but slaves of our wishes putting a bar to our advancement —
Thou are the only God and power to bring us up to that State.

Step 7

Repeat the prayer only once or twice—then broad over its meaning and guide into the condition it leads.

Step 8

A variety of thoughts may gain way over mind. Do not be afraid anyway, or irritated—do not try to control the thoughts—you shall fall if you attempt to do so.

Step 9

Do not bother to control thoughts as meditating—that is the job of the Master, not yours. Simply go on with the purpose.

Step 10

Sit thus for about an hour in the morning and evening to start; gradually increase to one hour with practice.

Step 11

Do not be carried away by "Divine or Master" visions; these items regarding visions on the spiritual path; you will be disappointed.

Step 12

Experiences will come as you travel, but they are incidental milestones—not the goal. Do not get lost on the road.

Step 13

Minutes are predicted by names and their terms materials; the goal is to go beyond names and mine to the purpose that meditation.

Step 14

Cleaning of the heart (evening meditation):

  • Sit for meditation as above.
  • Close your eyes and place attention on point (B), two finger widths above the left lower rib—below point (A). This location is the seat of "Mana," Across Dwellers.
  • Direct waves of divine grace over point (B) and let them leave toward your back, removing micro-impressions (Sanskara) from the casual body. Continue for 20 minutes.
  • You may slip into "Sat Samadhi," a twilight sleepy consciousness, during the process.
  • After cleaning, you will feel light in mind and spirit.
Step 15

Once your thoughts to the Master is gone by repeating the prayer once and Sat always gone on morning.

  • Draw your thought to the Master's grace by repeating the prayer once and Sat always
  • Say "Om Om Om" during the process.
  • The Maker knows to get expressed consciously during the night and experience new levels, always.
Step 16

Maintain harmonious, cordial relations with all. As a householder (Grihastha), responsibilities remain, and worries are natural, causing thought-disturbance. With practice, disturbances settle and detachment blooms—mind becomes calm and peaceful in the heart's depth. This follows the yogic principle of "detachment by attachment" taught by Lord Krishna in the Gita.

Step 17

Cultivate Trusteeship in household affairs—everyone is God's creation and you act as a trustee for their welfare; material means are provided by Him according to their Bhoga through you.

  • This Bhava gradually detaches you from action's effects; mind is freed from forming new Sanskaras—Nishkama Karma as in the Gita.
  • Thus this yogic practice is a practical compendium of the Gita's teaching.

Note: This method of meditation requires an initiation process from a IUSCM Preceptor for 30 minutes each day for 2 consecutive days either in-person or remotely. The IUSCM Preceptor is trained to clean the practitioner's spiritual centers (chakras) and insert the divine power from Hiranyagarbha (Providence) preparing him / her for further evolution on the plane of Consciousness.
